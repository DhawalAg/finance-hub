# Future Horizons: Search → Conversation → Prediction → Embedding

Search becomes conversational:
Right now search is a single shot — you type a query, get results, done. With ambient intelligence (agents across all your devices and contexts), search becomes a dialogue. You don't "search" anymore, you talk to a system that has persistent memory, knows your history, and refines iteratively. The agentic control loop we studied — query → retrieve → evaluate → refine — becomes invisible to the user. It just feels like a conversation.
Conversations become predictive:
Once the system has enough context from your ongoing conversations (cross-session memory, behavioral signals, device context), it stops waiting for you to ask. It starts anticipating. "You're opening your IDE at 9am on Monday — here are the PRs that need your review and the docs relevant to that bug you were debugging Friday." This is [[Anticipatory Search]] — the system models your intent before you express it. The conversation shifts from reactive to proactive.
Predictive becomes embedded:
This is the most speculative leap. "Embedded" here means the intelligence becomes part of the environment itself — not a tool you invoke, but a layer of reality you inhabit. Search is no longer an action. It's a property of your information environment. The system doesn't predict what you'll ask — it has already woven the answer into your context before the question forms. Think: AR glasses that surface relevant information as you look at something, or a coding environment that restructures itself around what you're about to do.
The key insight: each transition is about reducing the gap between intent and information:
 
  1. Search — you express intent explicitly, system retrieves
  2. Conversation — you express intent naturally, system reasons iteratively  
  3. Prediction — system infers intent from context, retrieves proactively
  4. Embedding — there is no gap; information and context are unified
This is what he means by "the better search gets, the less it resembles search" — the [[Search Paradox]]. At the end of this progression, there's nothing recognizable as "search" left. It's just... intelligence in the environment.
